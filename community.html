Community Guidelines

The app is designed around one question: what is happening next? These guidelines keep the feed relevant, safe, and useful.

1. The Core Focus: What Is Happening Next?

FOMO NOMO is built exclusively for discovering, sharing, and organizing upcoming meetups and events. Every post should provide actionable value about an upcoming activity.

2. Prohibited Content

  • Explicit Partying and Illegal Acts: No promotion of illegal activities, extreme intoxication, or explicit party culture.
  • Sexual Content: No explicit sexual material, nudity, or suggestive content.
  • Offensive Material: Harassment, hate speech, bullying, threats, and abusive language are not tolerated.
  • Harassment or targeting of specific individuals.
  • Impersonation of another student, organizer, or institution.
  • Attempts to circumvent the campus email verification process (e.g., sharing or selling .edu access).

More Prohibited Content

  • Self-Promotion and Advertisements: Don’t use the public feed for commercial ads, spam, or irrelevant self-promotion.
  • Off-Topic Content: Random musings, memes, and unrelated discussions will be removed.

3. Enforcement and Moderation

To report a user, post, or comment, tap the Report option available on all user-generated content.

Every post, comment, and profile has an in-app Report option. Reports are reviewed by our moderation team within 24 hours. Blocked users cannot view or interact with your content. Repeat or severe violations will follow an escalation path resulting in content removal, temporary suspension, or permanent account termination.
